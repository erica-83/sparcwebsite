Advocate for SPARC

Your voice matters. Help us secure funding for community-based day services that serve adults with severe and multiple disabilities in Virginia.

Why This Matters Now

Virginia has a legal obligation to provide integrated, community-based services. SPARC is already delivering what the Commonwealth needs.

Legal Compliance

Virginia is under a Permanent Injunction in United States v. Virginia requiring the state to provide integrated, community-based services for people with disabilities.

Proven Model

SPARC is not a pilot or proposal - it's an established, fully operating program already delivering the exact services Virginia is legally required to provide.

Cost Effective

SPARC operates at substantially lower cost than Medicaid-funded day programs while providing significantly higher quality programming and community integration.

A Strategic Path to Compliance

Funding SPARC is a direct, measurable, and cost-effective way for Virginia to demonstrate progress toward compliance with the ADA and the Permanent Injunction. Our participants are the exact population Virginia has struggled - over decades - to serve appropriately.

Dear , (e.g., Senator Smith, Delegate Jones, Supervisor Lee)

I am writing as the parent of an adult son/daughter with severe and multiple disabilities who lives in your district. I am asking you to support the SPARC (Specially Adapted Resource Centers) budget amendment currently before the General Assembly.

SPARC provides integrated, community-based day services for adults with complex disabilities who are unable to work and require continuous one-to-one personal care support. This is the exact population Virginia has struggled - over decades - to serve in compliance with the Americans with Disabilities Act and the Permanent Injunction issued against Virginia in United States v. Virginia.

SPARC is not a pilot or a proposal. It is an established, fully operating program already delivering the integrated services the Commonwealth is legally required to provide - at substantially lower cost than Medicaid-funded day programs and with a significantly higher level of programming, including richer community integration, individualized engagement, and meaningful daily activities that far exceed the programs currently serving adults with complex needs.

Funding SPARC is a strategic, direct, and cost effective way for Virginia to demonstrate measurable progress toward compliance with the Permanent Injunction.

I urge you to support this budget amendment and help ensure that adults with severe disabilities in Virginia have access to the quality community-based services they deserve.

Sincerely,
